 export (procedure directive)
 ------------------------------
The export directive belongs to the same family of procedure directives as
the near, far, inline, and interrupt directives.

The export directive makes a procedure or function exportable by
  - forcing the routine to use the far call model
  - generating special procedure entry and exit code

If procedures and functions are to be exported by a DLL, they must be
compiled with the export procedure directive. This means that an export
directive, if present, must be specified on the first introduction of a
procedure or function; it cannot be supplied in the defining declaration
of a forward declaration.

The actual export of the procedure or function does not occur until the
procedure or function is listed in a library's exports clause.
