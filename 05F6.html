 forward (procedure directive)
 -------------------------------
With a forward declaration, you can make a procedure or function known
without actually specifying its statement part.

From the point of the forward declaration, other procedures and functions
can call the forwarded routine, making mutual recursion possible.

Somewhere after a forward declaration, the procedure or function must be
defined by a declaration that specifies the statement part of the routine.

The defining declaration can omit the parameter list from the procedure or
function header.

 Example:
 (* Forwarded procedure *)
 procedure Flip(N: Integer); forward;

 procedure Flop(N: Integer);
 begin
   WriteLn('Flop');
   if N > 0 then Flip(N - 1);
 end;

 procedure Flip;
 begin
   WriteLn('Flip');
   if N > 0 then Flop(N - 1);
 end;
