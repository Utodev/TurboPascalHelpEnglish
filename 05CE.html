   Numbers
 ---------
 Ordinary decimal notation is used for numbers that are constants of type
 integer and real.

 A hexadecimal integer constant uses a  dollar sign ($) as a prefix.

 Engineering notation (E or e, followed by an exponent) is read as "times 10
 to the power of" in real types. For example:

 7E-2  means  7 x 10-2
 12.25e+6  or  12.25e6  both mean  12.25 x 10+6.

 Numbers with decimals or exponents denote real-type constants.

 Other decimal numbers denote integer-type constants; they must be between
 -2,147,483,648 and 2,147,483,647.

 Hexadecimal numbers:
Hexadecimal numbers denote integer-type constants; they must be between
$00000000 and $FFFFFFFF.

The resulting value's sign is implied by the hexadecimal notation.
